Computer Science as a Discipline and the Five Computing Disciplines

Computer Science as a Discipline

  • Computer science is a discipline that focuses on the study of algorithms, information sciences and applications for the use of automation. Computer science is heavily linked with the field of mathematics which is applied to most aspects of computing (Knuth, 1974).
  • Computer Science is a considered to be a Hard-applied field which implies that the field is primarily pragmatic and mainly involves one's creativity in making decisions (Clark, 2003).


BRIEF HISTORY

  • The history of computer science originated from the study of mathematical concepts which turned and applied into automated computing. Now computer science is an essential part of today's society and is considered to be one of the most influential fields of our century and has been commonly applied to military sciences and development (Dagupta, 2014; Edwards, 1995).


HISTORY
  • The history of computer science spans from ancient tools like the abacus, developed around 2700-2300 BCE, to significant contributions by Greeks and Indians such as Pāṇini's grammar and Al-Khwarizmi's algebra (Bhate & Kak, 1991; Mehri, 2017; Maričić & Lazić, 2020).
  • The 17th century brought mechanical innovations with Blaise Pascal's calculator and Leibniz's binary system, while the 19th century saw Charles Babbage's design for the analytical engine and Ada Lovelace's pioneering work in programming (Huskey & Huskey, 1980).
  • The 20th century saw rapid advancements, including Alan Turing’s theoretical groundwork and the ENIAC, the first electronic digital computer (Strickland & Lewis, 2022; Atkinson, 2014.)
  • Computer science became formalized as an academic discipline in the 1950s, with early degree programs established in Cambridge and Purdue. The late 20th century introduced personal computers, the internet, and new technologies like microprocessors and graphical interfaces.
  • Today, computer science encompasses fields such as AI, machine learning, and data science, continually evolving and shaping modern society (King & Roberts, 2018).

5 COMPUTING DISCIPLINES

Information Systems

  • Information Systems as a field involves mainly decision making processes with the use of data analytics (Amaravadi, 2022).
  • Information Systems involves the use of certain systems depending on the level/position of the users e.g MIS, EIS, DMS (Paul et al., 2020).
  • Information Systems focuses on data analytics and software that are used to support organizational operations of businesses. IS may also involve the The current climate for IS jobs will mainly comprise of analytical roles as well as managerial roles of companies (Piccol & Pigni, 2019).

Information Technology

  • Information Technology is the study of systematic approaches to develop, organize and administer technological solutions in order to achieve the goals of an individual or an organization. The IT industry is growing in a fast pace and has played an important role in today's society (Association of Computing Machinery, 2017).
  • Information Technology Graduates can get into a wide variety of careers in industries such as computer systems design, software development, computer systems analytics, computer support specializations, and Cybersecurity (Csorny, 2013).

Computer Science

  • Computer science is, in general, the study of computers, and their underlying principles and use (Daintith & Wright, 2008). Specifically, computer science focuses on algorithmic designs, processes and computing methods (Knuth, 1974).
  • Graduates of computer science can get into most computing fields such as software development, computer research and design, artificial intelligence computing and so much more (Winston, 1992; Rothmuller, 1978).

Computer Engineering

  • Computer Engineering is the field that focuses on the design of hardware systems and hardware components of Computer devices. The field of Computer Engineering is in charge of the production, manufacturing, packaging and optimization of hardware devices (Bell et al., 2014).
  • Computer engineering (CoE or CpE) integrates various domains such as hardware engineering, software engineering, and networking. Professionals in this field are trained in electronic engineering, computer science, and hardware-software integration, allowing them to work on both the physical components of computers and the software that runs on them. The Coverage of Computer Engineering may include Hardware design, Software development, Robotics, AI, and Networking (Khosla et al., 1995).

Software Engineering

  • Software Engineering is a field that utilizes engineering principles to develop efficient and economical software. Software Engineering aims to produce maintainable and good quality software in a set deadline and budget (Aggarwal, 2005).
  • If you are well versed in the field of software engineering, then you Jobs such as being a software architect, software designer, systems analyst, programmer, and software tester (Ahmed et al., 2012).
