Data and Information, Information Systems, and Different Types of Support Systems in Information Systems

Data

  • Basic, unprocessed, normally unfiltered information.
  • Data are facts that can be processed into a more comprehensive and readable format which is also known as Information (Liew, 2007).

   Example of Data:

Name Price Order ID Order Option
Fern Rosello P 28000 orderid233 opt2ordr


Information

  • Information is data that is useful after it has undergone a form of analysis (Liew, 2007). Information can also be described as a form of quantifiable intelligence that can be utilized. (Love, 2018)
  • Information is described to be data that has undergone processing and been given context.(Dammann, 2018).
  • Example of Information: “Fern Rosello ordered the least popular color of the latest gaming rig from Shopee.”


Information Systems

  • An Information System is a system that involves to facilitate or manage organizational processes within a company. (Watson, 2007)
  • Information systems (IS) use a range of information technologies (IT), including computers, software, databases, communication systems, the Internet, mobile devices, and much more, to perform certain activities, interact with, and inform different parties in various organizational or societal contexts. The goal of IS is to facilitate the information needs of a company that would help achieve the specific goals of all the parties in an organization. (Boell & Cecez, 2015)



Support Systems of IS






Transactional Processing System

  • Transaction Processing Systems are operational-level systems at the base of the pyramid. They are often operated directly. by shop floor workers or front line staff, who give the key data essential for operations management. This data is typically gathered through the automated or semi-automatic tracking of low-level activities and basic transactions (Muslihah, & Nastura, 2020).


Management Information System

  • A management information system is a type of information system that is used in an organization to make decisions as well as to coordinate, control, analyze, and visualize data. Management information systems includes people, processes, and technology in an organizational environment. This is mainly utilized by middle level Managers (Nayak et al., 2012)


Decision Support System

  • A decision support system (DSS) is an Information System that is used to support determinations, judgments, and courses of action in an organization or a business. This type of Information System is typically utilized by Senior-level managers. (Fang, 2009)


Executive Information System

  • An executive information system (EIS) is a specialized management support system designed to facilitate decision-making for senior executives within an organization. Also known as an executive support system (ESS), an EIS provides top-level management with easy access to both internal and external information that is crucial for strategic decision-making. (Leidner, 1993)


Office Automation System

  • Office automation systems (OAS) are designed to enhance efficiency and productivity in the workplace by utilizing technology to automate repetitive tasks. (Pathak et al., 2018)


Expert System

  • An Expert System is a system that utilizes the new technology of Artificial Intelligence to simulate decision making capabilities and help with information needs of an organization (Mohammed et al., 2019).
